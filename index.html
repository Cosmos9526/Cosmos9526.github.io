Click Chemistry

Click chemistry is a class of biocompatible reactions that are fast, selective, and produce high yields. These reactions are widely used in drug discovery, diagnostics, and bioconjugation. The most common click reaction is the azide-alkyne cycloaddition, useful for labeling biomolecules without disrupting biological systems.

Vaccine Design

Computational vaccine design uses tools like epitope mapping, molecular docking, and immunoinformatics to predict and optimize antigens. This approach speeds up development, especially for mRNA and peptide-based vaccines targeting viruses and cancer antigens.

Peptide Design

Peptide design involves predicting stable, functional sequences for therapeutic or diagnostic use. Algorithms model 3D folding and interaction with target proteins, helping create peptides that mimic natural ligands or inhibit disease pathways with precision.

Molecular Dynamics Simulation

Molecular dynamics simulations provide time-resolved, atomistic views of molecular systems. They're essential in drug design, protein folding studies, and binding interaction predictions. Simulations are performed with tools like GROMACS, AMBER, or NAMD.

Genetic

In computational genetics, bioinformatics tools analyze genomic sequences, predict gene functions, and simulate genetic variants. CRISPR target prediction, GWAS, and transcriptomics analysis are part of this field, helping uncover mechanisms of disease.
